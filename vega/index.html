The Compute Unlock

Cooling isn't the cost.

Power is already sold

At sites where power is already contracted and distribution is in place, thermal capacity becomes the binding constraint. The power is real. The bus is energized. The tenant has a contract.

Cooling is the constraint

Without thermal removal at 50–100 kW per rack, contracted power cannot support GPU load. Every idle kilowatt is stranded revenue — power the operator has sold but cannot deliver because air-cooled infrastructure was never designed for this density.

Revenue activates when thermal capacity does

Vega doesn't reduce operating cost. It converts contracted power into billable compute. The tenant goes live. The operator's timeline compresses from a capital construction project to a deployment event. The product pays for itself in weeks, not years.
