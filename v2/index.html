The Platform

Facility-to-Chip™
Cooling Ecosystem

An end-to-end architecture linking facility-level cooling infrastructure directly to chip-level heat removal — enabling precise thermal management, maximized efficiency, and simplified scalability from a single intelligent platform.

Facility Side

Chillers, fluid coolers, and mechanical refrigeration assets form the primary loop — connecting your existing facility infrastructure to the Borealis CDU.

Core Intelligence

The Borealis™ CDU serves as the brain — dynamically managing both loops, optimizing thermal envelopes, and integrating AI-driven predictive maintenance.

Chip Side

ThermaPod™ modules provide rack-level immersion, spray, or direct-to-chip cooling — tailored precisely to workload density.

Capabilities

Explore the Key Features
of the Borealis™ CDU Platform

Innovative modular designs ensure efficient thermal management, reducing energy consumption and enhancing the reliability of critical computing infrastructure.

High-Efficiency Cooling

Efficient cooling tailored for power-dense computing environments, ensuring optimal performance and reliability with reduced energy consumption compared to traditional methods.

Dual-Loop Flexibility

Supports both single-phase glycol and two-phase dielectric refrigerant loops in the same chassis. Switching modes requires only a heat exchanger swap — not a full hardware replacement.

Modular & Scalable

Easy integration and scalability adapting to your specific needs. Flexible operation modes support both single-phase and two-phase cooling for various applications.

Telemetry & Control

Advanced telemetry and intelligent control at row scale, delivering fast transient stability during workload spikes and real-time observability across both facility and IT cooling loops.

High-Efficiency Pumping

Industry-leading motor efficiency (>92%), built-in VFDs, and peer-to-peer load sharing for up to six units without an external PLC — maintaining operation near BEP for minimal parasitic load.

Future-Ready

A clean migration path from today's single-phase glycol systems to tomorrow's two-phase dielectric solutions — without parallel infrastructure or disruptive retrofits.
